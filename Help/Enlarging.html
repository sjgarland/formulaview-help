Enlarging the Views

Widening the Formula View pane

Sometimes the Formula View pane is not wide enough to display the entire tree view. In Excel for Windows or the Mac, users can hover the cursor over its left edge and drag it left to widen the pane.

Widening the Formula View pane

Undocking the Formula View pane

In Excel for Windows, users can make the Formula View pane even wider by first dragging its top edge left to undock the pane and then resizing the undocked pane by dragging its edges outward.

Undocking the Formula View pane
Enlarging the undocked pane

Creating a floating Formula View canvas

Excel for the Mac limits the width of an undocked Formula View pane, and Excel for the web does not allow changing it width or undocking it. To overcome these limits, the menu in the Formula View pane enables users to display that pane in a floating canvas with no limits on its width.

Floating the Formula View pane

Zooming in the Formula View pane or canvas

Excel provides zoom controls in its View ribbon and in its status bar, but these controls apply only to the worksheet and not to the Formula View pane. However, the native PC or Macintosh zoom controls can be used in that pane. On a PC, use Control +/- to zoom in or out. On a Macintosh, use Command +/-.
